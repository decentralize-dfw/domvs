The Istanbul Market

Ottoman heritage,
continental scarcity.

The Bosphorus waterfront is among the most geographically finite luxury corridors on earth. A total navigable coastline of approximately 29 kilometres, strict height restrictions protecting the skyline, and a legal heritage designation system that prevents demolition of significant structures — together these create supply constraints that have no equivalent in Western Europe.

MERGVS brings immersive spatial computing to Istanbul's prime market, where Ottoman-era yalı mansions, modern Bosphorus-view apartments, and historic Beyoğlu buildings require presentation that captures the spatial drama that photography cannot convey.

Bosphorus Waterfront (Yalı)
Historic waterfront mansions — the most finite and highest-valued residential asset class in Turkey. Ottoman-era timber construction with direct water access.
Beşiktaş & Ortaköy
European-side prime residential with direct Bosphorus views. High-specification apartment buildings alongside Victorian-era konak houses.
Beyoğlu & Nişantaşı
Istanbul's cultural and commercial heartland. 19th-century European-style architecture undergoing careful renovation for the city's creative and professional class.
Çengelköy & Asian Shore
The quieter Asian shore increasingly preferred by buyers seeking Bosphorus views with distance from European-side density. Historic villages preserved by conservation status.
